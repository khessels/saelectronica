Reverse-pulse electroplating machine for PCB development

Power electronics we designed and built in-house, and now use in our own board production.

Power electronics H-bridge · 24V / 10A STM32G474 In-house PCB design

What it does

When electroplating a PCB, the electrical pattern used during the process matters just as much as the chemistry: a poorly controlled pulse produces an uneven or poor-quality copper deposit. This machine generates and controls that pattern — including reverse pulses — so the plating process is precise and repeatable.

The board shown is the final result we designed and built: it includes its own power stage (H-bridge with up to 10A output at 24V), multi-rail voltage regulation (+24V, +5V, +3V3, and a negative -5V rail for the op-amps), real-time voltage and current feedback to control the process, and an STM32G474 microcontroller that coordinates the entire cycle.

Earlier project

High-resolution programmable power supply, 0–12V

A digitally controlled bench power supply, with software-based voltage and current adjustment and measurement.

Bench power supply 0–12V programmable ESP32 control High resolution

What it does

This power supply lets you set and measure output voltage and current digitally, instead of with manual potentiometers. An ESP32 module (WEMOS LOLIN32) governs the setpoint and reads the actual measurement for both voltage and current, allowing high-resolution control over the 0–12V output.

The design includes its own precision reference for the set and measure circuits, a -5V rail generated with a switched-capacitor voltage inverter (ICL7660) to power the op-amps, and +3V3 regulation for the control logic. Output current is measured via a shunt resistor.
